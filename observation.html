Observations

Variable Lab

In this lab, students are required to find short period variables from the AAVSO catalog. The figure shows the result of my attempt of recording the light curve of a SX Phoenicis variable with a period of 0.0390976 days (56.3005 min) and change in brightness of 0.33 magnitude (V band). 40x60s exposures with 1 minute cadence were taken in mid Dec of 2018. The data was reduced and analyzed with AstroimageJ. The light curve is then extracted using relative photometry, with 3 different reference stars in the field of view. The result is pretty satisfactory that the measured period and change in brightness are both very close to the documented values. Note that the absoluted values of the V-band magnitudes are not correct because the filter used is not standard photometric V filter.

Radio Lab

The radio lab will use the SPIDER 300A (a 3-meter dish and a 1.42 GHz receiver). It has just been installed in June 2019 (see the time-lapse of the installation below). So far, I am mostly focuing on testing the telescope and learning how to use the software for operating it. The figure in the left shows a 17*17 points mapping of the quiet sun, without flux calibration, yet.

Spectrograph Lab

The plot shows the spectrum of Sulafat taken with SA200 slit-less grating, corrected for instrument response and a linear dispersion. The red line is the data with the non-visible range excluded, and the grey line is a template spectrum of B9III star.
