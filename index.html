Ongoing works

After finishing my M.Phil. degree in Astrophysics, I was employed as a research assistant to develop an observation-only astronomy laboratory course for students in HKU. At the same time, I was also working to finalize and publish the work I have done during the M.Phil. degree.

Galaxy evolution and star formation

This is a project to probe star formation history in the BCG of a galaxy cluster MACS J0329, as the CLASH survey had detected filamentary structures from UV to IR. The figure shows that I have modeled the elliptical radial light profile and separated the filamentary structures (i.e. rows:original data, model, ratio image and subtracted image(residual); columns:near UV, optical and near IR filter images). Click the figure to see more about this project.

Observation course development

My challenge is to come up with observation plans that are feasible in a highly developed coastal city, Hong Kong. Then, verify these plans by putting them into practice. Finally, I have drafted the lab manuals to guide students developing their own observation proposals.
